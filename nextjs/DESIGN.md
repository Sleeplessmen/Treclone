# Design System Specification: The Focused Architectural Kanban

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: The Digital Curator**

This design system moves beyond the "utilitarian grid" of traditional project management. Instead, it treats the Kanban board as a high-end editorial gallery. The goal is to reduce cognitive load not just through "simplicity," but through **intentionality**. We achieve a premium, custom feel by favoring structural depth and tonal shifts over the rigid, line-heavy layouts of the past.

By leveraging an asymmetrical approach to whitespace and a sophisticated "No-Line" philosophy, the interface feels like it was curated by a designer, not generated by a database. We replace heavy borders with soft layering, creating a workspace that feels calm, authoritative, and profoundly focused.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy

The palette is rooted in a "Warm Neutral" foundation, using subtle shifts in luminance to define the workspace.

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Strict Mandate:** Designers are prohibited from using 1px solid borders to section off columns or headers. 
*   **How to define boundaries:** Use background color shifts. A Kanban column should be defined by `surface_container_low` sitting on a `background` floor. 
*   **The Transition:** Boundaries are felt, not seen, through the transition from `surface` to `surface_container`.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a physical stack of premium cardstock.
*   **Level 0 (The Floor):** `background` (#f7f9fb) – The base of the entire application.
*   **Level 1 (The Columns):** `surface_container_low` (#f0f4f7) – Use this for the Kanban column backgrounds to create a "recessed" feel.
*   **Level 2 (The Cards):** `surface_container_lowest` (#ffffff) – Tasks and cards sit on top, appearing to catch the most light.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
To elevate the "Modern" brand personality, floating elements (Modals, Hovering Task Previews) must use Glassmorphism:
*   **Color:** `surface` at 80% opacity.
*   **Effect:** `backdrop-blur: 12px`.
*   **Signature Texture:** Main Action Buttons (e.g., "New Task") should use a subtle linear gradient from `tertiary` (#0053dc) to `tertiary_container` (#3e76fe) at a 135-degree angle to provide "soul" and depth.

---

## 3. Typography: Editorial Authority

We use a dual-typeface system to balance professional rigor with modern elegance.

*   **The Voice (Manrope):** Used for `display` and `headline` tiers. The geometric nature of Manrope provides the "Modern" edge. Use `headline-sm` (1.5rem) for Column Titles to give them an authoritative, editorial presence.
*   **The Engine (Inter):** Used for `title`, `body`, and `label` tiers. Inter's high legibility ensures that even dense task descriptions remain readable.
*   **Hierarchy Tip:** Contrast a `title-md` (Inter, Bold) task name with a `label-sm` (Inter, Medium) metadata tag to create a clear scan path. Use "generous leading" (1.5x font size) for all body text.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth

### The Layering Principle
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Stacking**. To make a card "pop" from a column:
1.  Column Background: `surface_container_low`.
2.  Card Background: `surface_container_lowest`.
3.  **Result:** A natural, soft lift without a single drop shadow.

### Ambient Shadows
Shadows are reserved only for "Active" states (e.g., a card being dragged). 
*   **Spec:** `Y: 8px, Blur: 24px, Spread: -4px`.
*   **Color:** Use `on_surface` at 6% opacity. This mimics natural ambient light rather than a synthetic "drop shadow."

### The Ghost Border Fallback
If a border is required for accessibility (e.g., Input Fields):
*   Use `outline_variant` at **20% opacity**.
*   **Never** use 100% opaque lines; they "trap" the eye and break the flow of the minimalist grid.

## 5. Components

### Kanban Cards
*   **Background:** `surface_container_lowest`.
*   **Corner Radius:** `md` (0.75rem / 12px).
*   **Spacing:** `3` (1rem) internal padding.
*   **Prohibition:** No divider lines between the task title and the metadata. Use a `spacing-2` (0.7rem) vertical gap instead.

### Functional Status Chips
Used to denote status (Blocked, Review, etc.).
*   **Blocked:** `error_container` background with `on_error_container` text.
*   **In Progress:** `tertiary_container` background with `on_tertiary_container` text.
*   **Done:** Use a subtle tint of green (custom) or `primary_fixed` for a "finalized" look.
*   **Shape:** `full` (9999px) for a soft, pill-like appearance.

### Primary Buttons
*   **Shape:** `md` (0.75rem).
*   **Color:** Signature gradient (see Section 2).
*   **Typography:** `label-md` (Inter, Semi-bold, All-caps with 0.05em tracking).

### Input Fields
*   **Background:** `surface_container_high` (#e1e9ee).
*   **State:** When focused, shift background to `surface_container_lowest` and apply the "Ghost Border."

### Vertical Workflows (Lists)
*   **Separation:** Forbid dividers. Use `spacing-1.5` (0.5rem) to separate list items. The "No-Line" rule applies here; the white space *is* the divider.

---

## 6. Do’s and Don’ts

### Do:
*   **Do** use asymmetrical margins. If a column has a 24px left margin, try a 32px top margin for a more bespoke, editorial feel.
*   **Do** use `surface_bright` to highlight the "active" or "hovered" column.
*   **Do** prioritize vertical rhythm over horizontal density. Give tasks "room to breathe."

### Don’t:
*   **Don't** use pure black (#000000) for text. Always use `on_surface` (#2a3439) to maintain the soft, premium feel.
*   **Don't** use 1px borders to separate the sidebar from the main board. Use a subtle shift from `surface_dim` to `background`.
*   **Don't** use standard "drop shadows" on every card. This creates visual "noise" that contradicts the minimalist goal. Rely on Tonal Layering first.
